The National Park Service

The National Park Service (NPS) is an agency of the federal government of the United States that manages all national parks, many national monuments, and other conservation and historical properties with various title designations. The United States Congress created the agency on August 25, 1916 through the National Park Service Organic Act. The NPS is an operating unit of the United States Department of the Interior. The agency is charged with a dual role of preserving the ecological and historical integrity of the places entrusted to its management while also making them available and accessible for public use and enjoyment. In 2018, the NPS employed approximately 12,363 employees who oversaw 423 units, of which 63 were designated national parks (62 of them are presented in the following maps).

Landsat image and Park Boundary

National Parks have been a great project to reserve and protect nature resources and landscapes in the US. This map is focused on help the users to gain basic knowledge of the national Park such as its size and basic landcover status. The national park data is from National Park Boundary by National Park Service. The Landsat Imagery is from Multispectral Landsat (Mature Support).
